OVERVIEW
Queen’s Online School offers a flexible and personalised GCSE (Key Stage 4) programme for students aged 14–16. The programme follows the British curriculum and allows students to study IGCSE subjects online through live, interactive lessons supported by expert teachers.

Students benefit from a balance of academic rigour, flexibility, and a supportive online learning environment, preparing them for further education, university, and future careers.

KEY FEATURES
- Live, interactive online lessons
- 24/7 access to recorded lessons
- Small class sizes (maximum 20 students)
- Personalised subject selection
- Subject specialist teachers
- Structured daily lessons and tutorials
- Strong pastoral care and wellbeing support
- Online clubs, events, and extracurricular activities

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

Core Subjects (Required)
- English Language
- Mathematics

Science Options
- Double Award Science (Biology, Chemistry, Physics combined – 2 GCSEs)
OR
- Separate Sciences (Biology, Chemistry, Physics – up to 3 GCSEs)

Additional Subjects (Choose 5 or more)
Students can select from a wide range of subjects:

- Art & Design (Fine Art)
- Business
- Further Mathematics
- Biology (separate)
- Chemistry (separate)
- Physics (separate)
- Geography
- History
- Computer Science
- Spanish
- French

Additional subjects can be added for an extra cost.

SUBJECT DETAILS

English Language
Focuses on analysing fiction and non-fiction texts, developing writing skills, and understanding language techniques.

Mathematics
Covers algebra, geometry, statistics, probability, and problem-solving with real-world applications.

Double Award Science
Provides a combined study of Biology, Chemistry, and Physics, leading to two GCSE qualifications.

Separate Sciences
Allows students to study Biology, Chemistry, and Physics individually for deeper subject knowledge.

Art & Design
Encourages creative expression through drawing, painting, sculpture, and mixed media.

Business
Covers entrepreneurship, marketing, finance, and business operations.

Further Mathematics
Advanced mathematical concepts including algebra, calculus, and higher-level problem-solving.

Biology
Explores living organisms, genetics, ecosystems, and human biology.

Chemistry
Covers atomic structure, chemical reactions, energy changes, and environmental chemistry.

Physics
Focuses on forces, energy, electricity, waves, and space science.

Geography
Studies ecosystems, climate change, urbanisation, and global development.

History
Explores key historical events, conflicts, and societal changes.

Computer Science
Covers programming, algorithms, data representation, and computational thinking.

Spanish and French
Develop language proficiency and cultural understanding through real-world topics.
