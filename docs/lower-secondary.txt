OVERVIEW
Queen’s Online School offers a comprehensive Lower Secondary (Key Stage 3) programme for students aged 11–14. The programme is designed to build strong academic foundations while developing critical thinking, independence, and a love for learning.

Delivered through live, interactive online lessons, the curriculum prepares students for GCSE/IGCSE studies while supporting their personal and social development in a flexible, supportive environment.

KEY FEATURES
- Live, interactive online lessons
- Small class sizes (up to 20 students)
- Subject specialist, UK-qualified teachers
- Structured daily timetable
- Weekly support sessions and drop-ins
- 24/7 access to recorded lessons
- Strong pastoral care and wellbeing support
- Global online community

SUBJECT DETAILS

English
Develops reading, writing, speaking, and analytical skills through a variety of texts including fiction, non-fiction, and poetry.

Mathematics
Covers number operations, algebra, geometry, ratios, percentages, and probability, building a strong mathematical foundation.

Science
Explores biology, chemistry, and physics topics including cells, energy, forces, and chemical reactions.

Citizenship and Wellbeing
Focuses on personal development, relationships, health, and responsible decision-making.

Art
Encourages creativity through drawing, painting, sculpture, and exploration of art history.

Computer Science
Introduces coding, algorithms, computational thinking, and the ethical use of technology.

Drama
Builds confidence, communication, and teamwork through performance and theatrical studies.

History
Explores key historical events and periods, developing analytical and research skills.

Geography
Covers physical and human geography, including climate, ecosystems, and global issues.

Spanish and French
Develops language skills (reading, writing, listening, speaking) alongside cultural understanding.

Music (Optional)
Covers performance, composition, and appreciation of different musical styles and traditions.

Religious Education (Optional)
Explores world religions, ethical philosophies, and moral issues to develop critical thinking and empathy.
